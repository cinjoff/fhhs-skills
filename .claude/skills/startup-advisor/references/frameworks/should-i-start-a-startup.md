# Should I Start a Startup?

## Decision Framework

### Readiness Signals (need 3+ of 5)
1. **Problem obsession** — You can't stop thinking about this problem, not just the solution
2. **Domain insight** — You know something about this space that most people don't
3. **Founder-market fit** — Your background gives you an unfair advantage here
4. **Risk tolerance** — You can handle 12-18 months of uncertainty financially and emotionally
5. **Execution ability** — You can build the first version yourself or have a co-founder who can

### Red Flags
- Starting because you want to "be an entrepreneur" (identity-driven, not problem-driven)
- Can't articulate why NOW is the right time
- Haven't talked to potential customers
- Treating it as a backup plan
- Financial situation requires immediate income

### Framework: The Regret Test
Ask: "In 10 years, will I regret not trying this?" If yes, the real question is timing, not whether.

### YC Wisdom
- "The way to get startup ideas is not to try to think of startup ideas. It's to look for problems." — Paul Graham
- The best founders are often reluctant — they start because they can't NOT start
- Side project → traction → quit your job is lower risk than leap-of-faith founding
