# Customer Development

## The Mom Test (Rob Fitzpatrick)
Rules for talking to customers without getting lied to:
1. Talk about their life, not your idea
2. Ask about specifics in the past, not generics about the future
3. Talk less, listen more

### Good Questions
- "What's the hardest part about [doing this thing]?"
- "Tell me about the last time that happened"
- "What solutions have you tried? What didn't work?"
- "How much time/money does this cost you?"
- "Who else should I talk to?"

### Bad Questions
- "Would you use a product that does X?" (hypothetical = useless)
- "How much would you pay for X?" (everyone underestimates)
- "Do you think this is a good idea?" (everyone says yes to be nice)

## Interview Process
1. Find 20-30 people in your target segment
2. 30-minute conversations, not surveys
3. Record (with permission) and take notes
4. Look for patterns across 10+ conversations
5. The insight is in the STORIES, not the opinions

## Validation Signals
- They describe the problem before you mention it
- They've spent money/time trying to solve it
- They ask when they can try your solution
- They offer to introduce you to others with the same problem
