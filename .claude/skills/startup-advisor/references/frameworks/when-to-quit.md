# When to Quit

## Kill Criteria (3+ = seriously consider stopping)
1. **No validated demand** after 6+ months of customer development
2. **Founder relationship broken** beyond repair
3. **Market disappeared** or shifted fundamentally
4. **Out of money** with no path to revenue or fundraising
5. **Health or relationship damage** that can't continue
6. **Better opportunity** with significantly higher expected value

## Not Kill Criteria (don't quit for these)
- First product version flopped (iterate)
- Competitor launched something similar (execution matters more)
- Investor said no (get 50 no's before concluding)
- Growth is slow (if retention is strong, you have something)
- It's hard (it's always hard)

## The Honest Conversation
Ask yourself:
- "If I started over today with what I know, would I start this?"
- "Am I persisting out of conviction or sunk-cost fallacy?"
- "What would I advise a friend in my exact situation?"

## Quitting Well
- Tell investors honestly and promptly
- Return remaining capital if possible (builds reputation)
- Document learnings
- Help team members land well
- Take real time off before the next thing
