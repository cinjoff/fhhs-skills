# Product-Market Fit

## What PMF Feels Like
- Users are pulling the product from you (not you pushing it)
- Word of mouth is your top acquisition channel
- Usage grows even when you stop marketing
- Users get angry when it's down
- You can't hire fast enough

## Measuring PMF
- **Sean Ellis test:** Ask "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?" — 40%+ say "very disappointed" = PMF
- **Retention curve:** Does it flatten? If users stick around after 30/60/90 days, you have something
- **NPS > 50** among core users
- **Organic growth rate** > 0 (growing without paid acquisition)

## Getting to PMF
1. Start with a narrow segment (10 users who LOVE it > 1000 who think it's okay)
2. Talk to users weekly — not surveys, real conversations
3. Measure activation (when do users first get value?) and retention (do they come back?)
4. Iterate fast — weekly releases minimum
5. Kill features that don't drive retention

## Common Mistakes
- Confusing growth with PMF (paid growth without retention is a leaky bucket)
- Broadening too early (serve one segment perfectly first)
- Building features instead of talking to users
- Measuring vanity metrics (signups) instead of engagement (weekly active users)
