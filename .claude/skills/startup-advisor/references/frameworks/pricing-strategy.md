# Pricing Strategy

## Core Principle
Price on value delivered, not cost incurred. The question isn't "what does it cost me?" but "what's it worth to them?"

## Pricing Models
| Model | Best For | Watch Out |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Freemium | Products with viral/network effects | Free tier cannibalizing paid |
| Free trial | Complex products needing aha moment | Trial too long = no urgency |
| Usage-based | Variable consumption (API, storage) | Unpredictable revenue |
| Flat subscription | Simple products, predictable value | Leaves money on table |
| Tiered | Products serving different segments | Too many tiers = confusion |
| Per-seat | Team collaboration tools | Discourages adoption |

## Pricing Process
1. **Research** — What do competitors charge? What do alternatives cost?
2. **Segment** — Different customers = different willingness to pay
3. **Anchor** — Your highest tier sets the reference point
4. **Test** — Price is never "right" — it's always an experiment
5. **Increase** — Most startups underprice. Raise prices and see what happens.

## Rules of Thumb
- B2B: charge 10x what it costs you (minimum)
- If nobody complains about price, you're too cheap
- Annual plans: 2 months free (17% discount) is standard
- Enterprise: always custom pricing (never show it publicly)
- Free → paid conversion: 2-5% is normal for freemium
