# Bootstrap vs Raise Funding

## Decision Framework

### Bootstrap When
- Market allows gradual growth (no winner-take-all dynamics)
- Revenue possible from day one (services, info products, tools)
- Low capital requirements (software, no hardware/inventory)
- You want full control and ownership
- Market is niche (VCs won't fund it, but it's profitable)

### Raise When
- Winner-take-all market (speed matters more than efficiency)
- High upfront costs (hardware, regulatory, marketplace chicken-and-egg)
- Network effects mean early scale = permanent advantage
- You need to hire a team before revenue
- Competitors are funded and moving fast

### Fundraising Basics
- **Pre-seed:** $500K-$2M, idea + team + early traction
- **Seed:** $2M-$5M, product + early users + retention signals
- **Series A:** $10M-$25M, proven unit economics + growth engine
- SAFE notes are standard for pre-seed/seed (YC template)
- Don't optimize for valuation — optimize for the right partner

### Common Mistakes
- Raising because you can, not because you need to
- Spending raised money on premature scaling
- Giving up too much equity early (keep 70%+ through seed)
- Not having 18 months of runway after raising
